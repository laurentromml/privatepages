The Customer Voice Gap Report

Powered by Tromml, in Pivotree's voice. A demand-gen tool that proves the product-data problem in a prospect's own customers' words, and keeps the catalog improving on a loop.

Summary

Pivotree's hardest sale is not the build itself. It is getting a prospect to accept that their product data is costing them sales before they will pay to fix it.

This partnership solves that by showing the prospect the problem in their own customers' words, then keeps solving it on a recurring loop.

A Pivotree customer provides field intelligence they already have: call transcripts, sales notes, or product reviews. Tromml analyzes how their buyers actually describe a product and compares it to how the product data describes it. The output is a short report, prioritized by SKU, that shows where the two have drifted apart and what to change. It starts on a small set of SKUs, the approved changes feed directly into SKU Build, and then it runs again as new customer context comes in.

For Pivotree, it is a sales tool that creates demand rather than asserting it, and a reason for the work to recur instead of ending at a one-time cleanup. Tromml runs the analysis on the backend. Pivotree keeps the brand and the customer relationship. The customer ends up with product content written the way their buyers actually shop.

The shared problem

Your team and ours are working the same problem from opposite ends. You have to convince a distributor or manufacturer that weak product data is leaking conversion, and a capabilities deck rarely convinces a skeptic. We see product leaders sitting on the exact language they need, in their own calls and reviews, with no one reading it.

The stakes are rising. As shoppers move to answer engines, product data is no longer read only by a human on a product page. It is read by ChatGPT, Gemini, and the retailer agents deciding which products to surface. Thin or off-voice product data does not just lower conversion, it leaves the product out of the answer. That is the urgency behind everything Pivotree already publishes about agentic commerce, and a customer-voice gap is what exposes it.

Customer voice, not just keywords

Most product-data work adds volume. More specs, more bullets, more keywords. Adding volume rarely helps, because the content still does not match how customers describe the product.

A professional buyer and a DIY buyer use different words for the same part, and the PIM often matches neither. The language that closes the sale already lives in sales calls, reviews, and the notes reps never log into the system. That is the demand-side context most enrichment never captures, because enrichment usually starts from manufacturer feeds and taxonomies. Tromml reads that language and reports, SKU by SKU, where the product data and the customer have diverged. Search and answer-engine visibility improve as a result, not as the goal.

The same analysis is useful well beyond the product page. It informs messaging, merchandising, how reps sell, and what marketing leads with.

What each party gets

Pivotree gets a demand-generation entry point and a recurring motion. The analysis runs on a prospect's real SKUs and produces a quantified case for fixing the catalog, which gives your AEs a reason to engage and a clear next step into SKU Build and your managed-data programs. Because customer language keeps changing, the work recurs rather than ending at a single project, which fits a managed program better than a one-time cleanup.

The customer gets product content in their buyers' language. That tends to mean fewer abandoned product pages, fewer wrong-fit returns, stronger discovery in both search and answer engines, and a data-backed answer to questions their team has debated internally for a long time.

Tromml stays on the backend as the analyst and the context-capture layer. You keep the brand and the relationship. We built this for the automotive aftermarket, and we do not compete with your PIM or cataloging work. We supply the demand-side input that makes it better.

How it works

  1. Capture. The customer sends the field intelligence they already have: call transcripts, sales notes, or reviews. One handoff, not an integration project.
  2. Analyze. Tromml compares how customers describe the product against the current product data, whether it lives in a PIM or not.
  3. Recommend. A short report, prioritized by SKU, covering what to change in the messaging and data and why it matters.
  4. Build. Approved changes flow into Pivotree's SKU Build and become the live content.
  5. Measure and repeat. Track the lift, pull in new customer context, and run it again. The catalog keeps improving instead of going stale.
