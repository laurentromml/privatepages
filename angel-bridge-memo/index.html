The barrier to enterprise AI adoption

By one widely cited 2025 study, roughly 95 percent of enterprise AI pilots never reach production. The models are not the problem. The data underneath them is. Most companies are trying to run AI on information that is scattered, incomplete, or stuck in people's heads, and an agent is only as good as the data it can actually reach.

Nowhere is that gap wider than in the aftermarket. The most valuable intelligence in the business lives in conversations between reps and customers, and almost none of it reaches a system. Reps walk out of shops every day knowing what is selling, what is slipping, and where the next order is hiding, and then it is gone. Managers coach off gut and stale CRM fields. This is a $435B channel, deeply fragmented, run on relationships and phone calls, with a sales force retiring fast and frontline CRM adoption stuck around 20 to 30 percent.

Here is why now. AI just crossed from giving advice to taking action, and it can finally pull the signal out of messy, spoken conversation and turn it into data an agent can use. The gap has been there for decades. AI is what makes closing it worth doing, and whoever captures and structures that data first becomes the system this industry runs on.

Minecart: activity intelligence for the aftermarket

We give managers visibility into the most important things happening across their accounts. We mine the data that is already there, rank it against the company's goals, and point managers straight to the opportunities and actions worth their time, so they can coach their reps instead of chasing them.

Information is only as good as its inputs, so we made capture take seconds. A rep leaves a quick voice memo or clips something from the browser, and that single capture becomes both a reminder for them and an executable follow-up opportunity in the system. No call reports, no forms. Then we monitor how those opportunities actually progress, so reps are not stuck checking boxes in the CRM to prove they did the work. The system watches the work instead of making the rep narrate it.

Every lap of that loop compounds. The reps get faster, the managers see more, and the data underneath gets richer, which is the real unlock for enterprise AI. None of it asks a company to rip out what it already runs on.
