About Auroraflow

Reimagining wellness
for the rest of us.

An LGBTQ-owned wellness spa in downtown Durham, built for the people the wellness world usually leaves out.

Our story

For most of its history, the wellness industry was built for a fairly narrow kind of person. If you didn't fit that picture, you learned to make yourself smaller to get through the door. We started Auroraflow because we were tired of watching people do exactly that.

So we built the place we wished existed. The intake form asks how you'd like to be addressed before it asks anything else. The tables don't have weight limits. There are ramps where other spas have steps. Staff wear their pronouns because it costs us nothing and means everything to the person reading the name tag.

That's really the whole idea. Your identity isn't something to explain here, your body isn't something to apologize for, and your time on the table belongs to you. We didn't set out to build a spa so much as a place where people could finally exhale.

Our Mission

"To facilitate accessible wellness experiences that improve the lives of our team, clients, and community, with safety, holistic care, and radical inclusion at the center of everything we do."

What we stand for

Everyone's actually welcome

Not "all are welcome" on a sign by the door: welcome in the way the room is built. Affirming intake, no weight limits on the tables, ramps where you'd expect stairs. If you've had to brace yourself walking into a spa before, that's the feeling we set out to remove.

Care shouldn't be a luxury

We run Pay What You Can days twice a month and keep membership pricing honest, because rest isn't a reward for the people who can already afford it.

We ask before we assume

Every session opens with a real check-in. Your preferences get written down and remembered, and "no" is a complete answer at any point, no explanation owed.

Our team comes first too

Fair pay, a culture that doesn't ask anyone to code-switch, and room to keep learning. People take better care of others when they're cared for, so we start there.

Rooted in Durham

607 William Vickers Ave sits in the heart of downtown Durham, a city that has always made room for the people the rest of the world has tried to push out. We chose Durham intentionally, and Durham has given back tenfold.

We partner with local organizations, host community events, and run Pay What You Can days every second and fourth Thursday to ensure our services are accessible regardless of financial circumstances.

Auroraflow was built by this community and for this community, and that will never change.
