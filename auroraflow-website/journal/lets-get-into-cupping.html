Let's Get Into Cupping! What Is It and How Can It Help You?

Experience cupping, the use of suction cups to safely release tension along your body. Non-invasive, completely safe, and deeply relaxing.

Auroraflow Team · 2 min read

You might have heard whispers about cupping before. Maybe you've seen those red circles on people's backs and were curious. It's time to unravel the mystery of this ancient therapy and dive into the incredible benefits it can bring to your day.

What is Cupping, Anyway?

Smooth, specially designed cups are placed on your skin, creating a gentle suction. This suction encourages blood flow to the treated areas, promoting healing and providing a soothing sensation. Cupping has been around for centuries, known to have originated from ancient Chinese, Egyptian, and Middle Eastern practices. At Auroraflow, our therapists are tested and certified in practicing it during their treatments.

At Auroraflow we use dry-cupping as our preferred method.

Why Should You Try Cupping?

Stress Relief: Cupping eases tension and helps you drift into a state of relaxation, like a mini-vacation for your body and mind.

Bye-bye Muscle Knots: Sitting in front of a computer or standing on your feet all day can lead to muscle knots and pain. Cupping helps to release muscle tension, making you feel as loose as a noodle.

Detoxification: Cupping helps to draw out impurities and toxins from deep within your muscles, leaving you feeling refreshed and revitalized.

Boosts Circulation: Cupping enhances blood circulation, promoting faster healing and a healthier body overall.

Immunity Boost: Cupping stimulates the lymphatic system, helping your body fight off infections more effectively.

Is Cupping Right for You?

Absolutely! Cupping is for everyone. This therapy is non-invasive and completely safe when performed by trained professionals. If you are concerned, our certified massage therapists are able to answer any and all questions you may have.

But Does It Leave Marks?

While cupping may leave temporary circular marks on your skin, they're just a sign that the therapy is doing its job! These marks usually fade within a few days.
